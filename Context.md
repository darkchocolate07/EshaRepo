## Project 1: Generative AI Benchmarking & Vendor Advisory

### Card summary

An empirical evaluation of Claude, ChatGPT, and Perplexity against human analyst baselines for enterprise IT vendor assessment.

### Challenge

Enterprise teams increasingly use LLMs to accelerate vendor research, but model-generated recommendations may vary in consistency, compliance coverage, analytical quality, and reliability.

### Approach

- Designed and executed an empirical benchmarking framework.
- Compared **Claude, ChatGPT, and Perplexity** with human analyst baselines.
- Evaluated **6 IT helpdesk vendors**.
- Assessed vendors against:
  - SOC 2
  - ISO 27001
  - PDPA
  - GDPR
- Evaluated model performance across:
  - Consistency
  - Analytical quality
  - Compliance coverage
  - Recommendation reliability

### Outcome

Identified important limitations in using LLM-generated outputs for enterprise vendor selection and demonstrated the need for human validation, transparent evaluation criteria, and governance controls.

### Suggested tags

## 16. Visual Direction

### Overall aesthetic

The site should feel:

- Premium
- Modern
- Minimal
- Intelligent
- Trustworthy
- Enterprise-ready
- Warm enough to feel personal

Avoid making the site look like:

- A generic student template
- A crowded resume pasted onto a webpage
- A futuristic AI landing page filled with neon effects
- A corporate consulting website with excessive jargon
- A portfolio that prioritises animation over readability

### Recommended colour direction

Use a restrained professional palette, for example:

- Deep navy or charcoal for authority
- Warm off-white for the main background
- Muted teal, blue, or violet as the accent
- Soft neutral borders
- High-contrast body text

The final palette should meet WCAG AA contrast requirements.

### Typography

Recommended combinations:

- Headings: Manrope, Sora, or Plus Jakarta Sans
- Body: Inter, Source Sans 3, or IBM Plex Sans

Use no more than two font families.

### Layout

- Large, confident hero section
- Generous spacing
- Clear typographic hierarchy
- Alternating experience and case-study layouts
- Rounded but restrained cards
- Thin borders rather than heavy shadows
- Maximum content width of approximately 1100-1200 px
- Comfortable reading width for long text
- Responsive mobile-first layout

### Imagery

Preferred:

- Professional portrait supplied by Esha
- Abstract data, systems, or strategic-framework visuals
- Simple custom diagrams for case studies
- Subtle geometric or grid-based background elements

Avoid:

- Random stock-office photos
- Generic robot imagery
- Overused glowing AI brain graphics
- Images that imply clients or employers not listed in the source material

### Motion

Use subtle motion only:

- Fade or slide on section entry
- Hover lift for project cards
- Animated underline or accent for navigation
- Smooth in-page scrolling

Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
